Daily Fuel

NUTRITION

Science-based phased nutrition. Calories drop each block as your body adapts. Every meal, every day, every week — all 12 weeks mapped out.

THE SCIENCE
TDEE ~3,002 kcal (moderate job + training). Protein set at 2.2g/kg (~194g/day) per Morton et al. (2018) for hypertrophy. Deficit increases progressively as the program progresses to maintain consistent rate of fat loss. Carbs concentrated around training; pre-bed casein/cottage cheese supports overnight muscle protein synthesis.
🍗
Protein Every Single MealSpread across 4–5 meals. Research shows this maximises muscle protein synthesis vs eating it all at once.
🥘
Meal Prep SundaysCook bulk chicken, rice, and chop veggies every Sunday so you never default to takeout.
🌶️
Season Your FoodSriracha, soy sauce, your fave spices — zero calories, massive flavour. Clean eating doesn't have to be boring.
🚫
Limit AlcoholSuppresses muscle protein synthesis for up to 24 hours. Minimise for 12 weeks.
🥡
If You Eat OutGrilled protein + salad or rice. Avoid fried and creamy. Once a week won't derail you.
⏱️
Pre + Post Workout MealsEat within 2–3 hours before and after training. Muscles are most primed to absorb nutrients at this window.

Morning with food

Improves sleep quality and recovery. Helps hit that 7–8hrs target.
The Other Stuff

LIFESTYLE

Training and food are 70% of the equation. The rest comes down to sleep, stress, and staying consistent when motivation dips.

😴
SLEEP
Target 7–9 hours every night. You're already in the right zone (~7h) — protect it like another training session. Phone out of room, hard cutoff 30 min before bed, consistent bed/wake times.

  • Set a consistent bedtime — same time every night
  • No screens 30 mins before bed if possible
  • Magnesium Glycinate before bed will genuinely help
  • Keep your room cool and dark
🧠
STRESS
Moderate stress is normal — what matters is recovery between hits. A few habits can keep cortisol in check across the week.

  • Training itself is one of the best stress relief tools — use it
  • A 10-minute walk after work helps the nervous system shift gears
  • 10 mins of deep breathing or a walk after work makes a difference
  • Don't skip rest days — they matter
📸
TRACK PROGRESS
The scale doesn't tell the whole story. Track these too:

  • How your clothes fit (especially waist)
  • Strength improvements week to week
  • Energy levels throughout the day
  • Weekly photos — same lighting, same pose, front and side
  • Sleep quality out of 10
📅
WEEKLY CHECK-IN
Every Sunday after meal prep:

  • Weigh yourself — morning, after toilet, before food
  • Take front and side photo in the same spot
  • Rate energy, sleep, and stress out of 10
  • Log it in the Tracker tab
  • Send to your trainer so program can be adjusted
Trust the process. Put in the work.
